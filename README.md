## Notable Events of 2008

| Event | Description |
|---|---|
| [Barack Obama elected US president](history-politics/barack-obama-election.md) | Democrat Barack Obama defeated Republican John McCain on November 4, becoming the first African American elected to the US presidency, a historic milestone in American political life. |
| [2008 Global Financial Crisis](society-economics/2008-financial-crisis.md) | The collapse of the US subprime mortgage market and cascading failures of financial institutions triggered the worst economic catastrophe since the Great Depression, with September 2008 marking the acute phase and reshaping global finance, regulation, and politics. |
| [Lehman Brothers collapse](society-economics/lehman-brothers-collapse.md) | On September 15, investment bank Lehman Brothers filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy — the largest in US history at the time — triggering a global financial panic and deepening the worldwide recession. |
| [Beijing Summer Olympics](history-politics/beijing-summer-olympics.md) | China hosted the Summer Olympic Games from August 8 to August 24, featuring record-breaking athletic performances and marking China's emergence as a global sporting and economic power. |
| [2008 Mumbai terrorist attacks](history-politics/mumbai-terrorist-attacks.md) | Coordinated attacks by Lashkar-e-Taiba gunmen struck hotels, a train station, and other sites in Mumbai from November 26–29, killing 166 people and straining India-Pakistan relations. |
| [Large Hadron Collider first beam](science-technology/large-hadron-collider-first-beam.md) | CERN's Large Hadron Collider circulated its first proton beam on September 10, inaugurating the world's largest and most powerful particle accelerator and opening a new era in high-energy physics. |
| [The Hunger Games published](arts-culture/the-hunger-games-novel.md) | Suzanne Collins published *The Hunger Games*, the first novel in a young adult dystopian trilogy, on September 14, establishing a publishing phenomenon that rivaled *Twilight* and defined young adult literature for a generation. |
| [The Dark Knight released](arts-culture/the-dark-knight.md) | Christopher Nolan's Batman sequel opened on July 18, earning over $1 billion worldwide and setting a new standard for the superhero genre; Heath Ledger's Joker performance won a posthumous Academy Award. |
| [SpaceX Falcon 1 reaches orbit](science-technology/spacex-falcon-1-orbital-launch.md) | On September 28, SpaceX's Falcon 1 became the first privately developed liquid-fueled rocket to reach Earth orbit, validating commercial spaceflight and securing a NASA cargo contract. |
| [Russo-Georgian War](history-politics/russo-georgian-war.md) | Russia launched a military intervention in Georgia on August 8, establishing itself as a resurgent military power and setting a precedent for later interventions in post-Soviet territories. |
| [Kosovo declares independence](history-politics/kosovo-declaration-of-independence.md) | Kosovo's assembly proclaimed independence from Serbia on February 17, the last state to emerge from Yugoslavia's breakup, dividing the world over self-determination and recognition. |
| [Cyclone Nargis strikes Myanmar](society-economics/cyclone-nargis.md) | The category-4 cyclone made landfall in Myanmar's Irrawaddy Delta on May 2–3, killing an estimated 138,000 people and exposing the junta's refusal to accept international disaster relief. |
| [2008 Sichuan earthquake](society-economics/sichuan-earthquake.md) | The magnitude-8.0 Wenchuan earthquake struck Sichuan, China on May 12, killing about 69,000 people and igniting a national controversy over collapsed "tofu-dreg" schools. |
| [Coldplay releases Viva la Vida](arts-culture/coldplay-viva-la-vida.md) | Coldplay released their fourth studio album, *Viva la Vida or Death and All His Friends*, on June 12, featuring experimental production by Brian Eno; the lead single became a global hit and the album won the 2009 Grammy Award for Best Rock Album. |
| [Bernie Madoff Ponzi scheme exposed](society-economics/bernie-madoff-ponzi-scheme.md) | On December 11, investment manager Bernard Madoff was arrested after confessing to a decades-long Ponzi scheme estimated at $64.8 billion — the largest securities fraud in US history at the time. |
| [TARP bailout signed](society-economics/tarp-bailout.md) | President George W. Bush signed the $700 billion Troubled Asset Relief Program into law on October 3, authorizing the US Treasury to purchase toxic assets and stabilize the collapsing financial system. |
| [Usain Bolt sets 100m world record](people/usain-bolt.md) | Jamaican sprinter Usain Bolt ran the 100 metres in 9.69 seconds at the Beijing Olympics on August 16, setting a world record and beginning his transformation into the sport's dominant figure. |
| [Twilight film released](arts-culture/twilight-film-2008.md) | Catherine Hardwicke's adaptation opened November 21, achieving the highest opening ever for a female-directed film and launching a multi-billion-dollar young adult franchise. |
| [Slumdog Millionaire released](arts-culture/slumdog-millionaire.md) | British director Danny Boyle's film opened in limited US release on November 12, following Dev Patel as a Mumbai teenager on a game show, later winning eight Academy Awards including Best Picture and marking a breakthrough for Indian cinema globally. |
| [2008 US Presidential debates](history-politics/2008-presidential-debates.md) | Three debates between Barack Obama and John McCain took place September 26–October 15, with performance differences shaping voter perceptions as the financial crisis deepened and influencing the November election outcome. |
| [Bitcoin whitepaper published](science-technology/bitcoin-whitepaper.md) | Pseudonymous developer Satoshi Nakamoto published the Bitcoin whitepaper on October 31, introducing peer-to-peer electronic cash and founding cryptocurrency and blockchain technology. |
| [Michael Phelps wins eight golds in Beijing](people/michael-phelps.md) | American swimmer Michael Phelps won eight gold medals at the Beijing Olympics in August, surpassing Mark Spitz's record and setting seven world records in one Games. |
| [Nastia Liukin wins Olympic all-around gold](people/nastia-liukin.md) | American gymnast Nastia Liukin won the women's all-around gold medal on August 15 with 63.325 points, defeating teammate Shawn Johnson by 0.6 points and becoming the first U.S. woman ever to win this Olympic title. |
| [Netflix expands to Xbox 360](science-technology/netflix-streaming-video.md) | Netflix launched its streaming service on Xbox 360 on November 19, offering over 10,000 titles on televisions and marking the critical moment streaming became accessible in living rooms, beginning the decline of physical media rental. |
| [Apple App Store launches](science-technology/apple-app-store-launch.md) | Apple opened the App Store on July 10 with about 500 third-party applications, creating the mobile app economy and turning the smartphone into a general-purpose computing platform. |
| [Google Chrome browser launches](science-technology/google-chrome-launch.md) | Google released the beta version of its web browser on September 2, introducing the V8 JavaScript engine and multi-process architecture that prioritized speed and stability, eventually becoming the world's dominant browser. |
| [WALL-E released](arts-culture/wall-e.md) | Pixar's animated science-fiction film, directed by Andrew Stanton, opened June 27 and won the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature, acclaimed for its near-silent storytelling and environmental themes. |
| [First Android phone launches (HTC Dream)](science-technology/htc-dream-android-launch.md) | The first commercially available phone running Google's Android, announced September 23 and sold as the T-Mobile G1, launched what became the world's dominant mobile platform. |
| [Rafael Nadal wins Wimbledon and Olympic gold](people/rafael-nadal.md) | Nadal beat Roger Federer in a July 6 Wimbledon final regarded as tennis's greatest match, then took Beijing Olympic gold and the world No. 1 ranking. |
