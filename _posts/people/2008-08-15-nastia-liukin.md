## Summary

Gymnast Nastia Liukin won the gold medal in women's individual all-around at the 2008 Beijing Summer Olympics on August 15, 2008, with a score of 63.325. She defeated American teammate Shawn Johnson by just 0.6 points in one of the closest all-around competitions in Olympic history. Liukin's victory made her the third American woman to win the Olympic all-around title, following Mary Lou Retton at the 1984 Los Angeles Olympics and Carly Patterson at the 2004 Athens Olympics; it was the second consecutive Games at which an American woman took the title. Her triumph extended a period of renewed American excellence in women's gymnastics and showcased the depth of talent in the U.S. women's team, which also medaled strongly in team and apparatus competitions at Beijing.

## Career Background and Path to Beijing

Nastia Liukin was born on December 30, 1989, in Moscow, Russia, the daughter of Valeri Liukin and Anna Kotchneva — both former Soviet gymnastics champions. The family immigrated to the United States in 1992 when Valeri accepted a coaching position in Texas. Trained exclusively by her father at World Olympic Gymnastics Academy (WOGA) in Plano, Texas, she became a U.S. citizen and rose through junior competition to dominate the senior international circuit.

Liukin's senior international career produced a remarkable haul before the Beijing Games: at the 2005 World Championships in Melbourne she won the all-around title and multiple apparatus medals, establishing her as the heir to American gymnastics. Between 2005 and 2008 she collected nine World Championship medals across all-around and apparatus events. Her style — recognized for exceptional artistry, extension, and difficulty on uneven bars — contrasted sharply with Shawn Johnson's more powerful, consistent approach, and the two Americans entered Beijing as co-favorites.

Valeri Liukin, as her coach, brought firsthand Olympic experience: he won three gold medals at the 1988 Seoul Olympics (men's team, parallel bars, and horizontal bar) as a member of the Soviet squad. The father-daughter partnership lent the 2008 victory a rare intergenerational dimension, with his own Olympic mastery directly informing his daughter's championship preparation.

## Beijing 2008 Medal Campaign

Liukin's performance at the 2008 Beijing Olympics was among the most decorated by any gymnast at a single Games. Her five-medal haul comprised:

- **Gold** — Individual all-around (August 15, score: 63.325)
- **Silver** — Uneven bars
- **Silver** — Balance beam
- **Silver** — Team all-around (with the U.S. women's gymnastics team)
- **Bronze** — Floor exercise

The five-medal total made Liukin the most decorated American gymnast at the 2008 Games and one of the most decorated at any single Olympics. Her uneven bars and balance beam performances were particularly praised for artistic expression and technical difficulty. The uneven bars final was the subject of a widely discussed judging controversy: Liukin and China's He Kexin finished with identical scores of 16.725, but He was awarded the gold and Liukin the silver under the sport's tie-breaking procedure, which averaged the counting execution deductions.

## Key Facts

- **Event and date**: Women's Individual All-Around, August 15, 2008, Beijing Summer Olympics
- **Score**: 63.325, defeating silver medalist Shawn Johnson (62.725) by 0.600 points
- **Historic achievement**: Third American woman to win the Olympic all-around gold, after Mary Lou Retton (1984 Los Angeles) and Carly Patterson (2004 Athens); the second consecutive Games won by an American woman
- **Father/Coach**: Valeri Liukin, three-time Soviet Olympic gold medalist (1988 Seoul) and co-founder of WOGA in Plano, Texas
- **Total 2008 medals**: 5 (1 gold, 3 silver, 1 bronze) — the most by any gymnast at the Beijing Games
- **Team context**: U.S. women's team won silver in the team all-around behind China
- **Close competition**: The 0.600-point margin between Liukin and Johnson remains among the tightest all-around finals in Olympic history
- **Age**: Competing at age 18, in her first and only Olympic Games as an active competitor

## Significance

Nastia Liukin's all-around victory made the United States the first nation to win back-to-back Olympic women's all-around titles since the Soviet era, following Carly Patterson's gold in 2004, and cemented the country's standing as a dominant force in the sport. The competition between Liukin and Johnson demonstrated the extraordinary depth of American women's gymnastics in 2008 — two of the world's top three gymnasts were American teammates.

Her win extended American gymnastics into an era where U.S. women had achieved parity with and occasionally exceeded the historical dominance of Soviet and post-Soviet gymnasts. The Beijing all-around final drew enormous television audiences in the United States, contributing to sustained public interest in gymnastics that helped grow the sport's domestic talent pipeline.

Having her father — himself a decorated Olympic gold medalist — as her coach added a remarkable intergenerational dimension. Valeri Liukin's expertise directly shaped a victory that occurred 20 years after his own Olympic triumph in Seoul, making Beijing 2008 a rare instance of an Olympic champion coaching an Olympic champion from within his own family.
