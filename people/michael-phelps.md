# Michael Phelps

**Category**: People  
**Key figures**: Michael Phelps (swimmer), Bob Bowman (coach), Jason Lezak (relay anchor), Milorad Čavić, Mark Spitz (previous record-holder)

## Summary

Michael Phelps is an American competitive swimmer who, at the 2008 Beijing Summer Olympics, won eight gold medals at a single Games — surpassing the record of seven set by Mark Spitz at the 1972 Munich Olympics. Competing between August 10 and August 17, 2008, the 23-year-old swimmer claimed gold in five individual events and three relays, setting world records in seven of the eight and an Olympic record in the eighth. The achievement is widely regarded as one of the most dominant individual performances in Olympic history.

Born on June 30, 1985, in Baltimore, Maryland, Phelps had already established himself as the sport's leading figure before Beijing. At the 2004 Athens Olympics he won six gold and two bronze medals, and in the years that followed he repeatedly lowered world records across butterfly, freestyle, and individual medley events. Coached throughout his career by Bob Bowman, Phelps entered the 2008 Games having publicly set the goal of matching or exceeding Spitz's mark — an objective that required winning every event he entered, including relays dependent on his teammates.

## Beijing 2008 Performances

Phelps won gold in the 400-metre individual medley, the 4×100-metre freestyle relay, the 200-metre freestyle, the 200-metre butterfly, the 4×200-metre freestyle relay, the 200-metre individual medley, the 100-metre butterfly, and the 4×100-metre medley relay. Seven of these victories were achieved in world-record time; the 100-metre butterfly was won in an Olympic record. Three races in particular defined the campaign:

**4×100-metre freestyle relay — August 11, 2008**: Anchoring for the United States, Jason Lezak overhauled France's Alain Bernard, the individual world-record holder, on the final length, touching the wall in 3:08.24 to the French team's 3:08.32 — a margin of 0.08 seconds and a world record. Lezak's anchor split of 46.06 seconds was the fastest relay leg in the event's history. The victory preserved Phelps's pursuit of eight golds, which a defeat in this event would have ended.

**200-metre butterfly — August 13, 2008**: Phelps won in a world-record time despite his goggles filling with water during the race, leaving him largely unable to see for much of the final length. He swam the closing portion by counting his strokes.

**100-metre butterfly — August 16, 2008**: In the closest of his individual finals, Phelps won in an Olympic-record 50.58 seconds, edging Serbia's Milorad Čavić by one hundredth of a second. Underwater photography and timing review confirmed that Phelps had reached the wall first.

Phelps secured his record-breaking eighth gold medal in the 4×100-metre medley relay on August 17, 2008, again in world-record time, surpassing Mark Spitz's 36-year-old mark of seven golds at a single Games.

## Significance

Michael Phelps's eight gold medals at the 2008 Beijing Olympics stand as the most gold medals won by any athlete at a single Olympic Games. The feat, combined with the seven world records set in achieving it, established Phelps as the central figure of the Beijing Games and one of the most decorated athletes in Olympic history. His campaign drew exceptional global television audiences and, together with Usain Bolt's three sprint world records, defined Beijing 2008 as a landmark Olympics for individual athletic achievement.

The narrow margins of several victories — most notably the relay anchored by Jason Lezak and the one-hundredth-of-a-second win in the 100-metre butterfly — underscored how close the record came to falling short, and contributed to the performance's lasting prominence in sporting memory. Phelps continued competing through the 2012 and 2016 Olympics, ultimately retiring as the most decorated Olympian of all time, with 28 medals including 23 golds.
