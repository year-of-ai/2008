# Rafael Nadal

**Category**: People  
**Key figures**: Rafael Nadal (tennis player), Roger Federer (rival), Fernando González (Olympic final opponent), Toni Nadal (coach)

## Summary

Rafael Nadal is a Spanish professional tennis player whose 2008 season transformed him from a clay-court specialist into the sport's dominant all-surface player and its world No. 1. Across the year he won the French Open, claimed his first Wimbledon title in a final widely regarded as the greatest match in tennis history, and captured the Olympic singles gold medal at the Beijing Games — a combination that ended the five-year reign of Roger Federer at the top of the men's game.

Born on June 3, 1986, in Manacor, Mallorca, Spain, and coached throughout his early career by his uncle Toni Nadal, he had by 2008 already won the French Open three consecutive times (2005–2007) but had never won a major on grass or hard courts and had spent a record 160 consecutive weeks ranked No. 2 behind Federer. The 2008 season resolved that gap: on August 18, 2008, following his Olympic victory, Nadal became world No. 1 for the first time, displacing Federer, who had held the position for a record 237 consecutive weeks.

## 2008 Season

**French Open — June 8, 2008**: Nadal won his fourth consecutive French Open title, defeating Federer in the final 6–1, 6–3, 6–0. The result was among the most one-sided finals in Grand Slam history and extended Nadal's dominance on clay, where he had not lost a completed match at Roland Garros since his debut in 2005.

**Wimbledon — July 6, 2008**: Facing Federer, who had won the previous five Wimbledon championships (2003–2007), Nadal won 6–4, 6–4, 6–7(5), 6–7(8), 9–7 in a match lasting 4 hours and 48 minutes — at the time the longest singles final in Wimbledon history. Interrupted twice by rain and concluding in near-darkness, the final saw Federer save two championship points in the fourth-set tiebreak before Nadal secured victory in the deciding set. It was Nadal's first Wimbledon title and his first Grand Slam title away from clay, and it is frequently cited as the greatest tennis match ever played.

**Beijing Olympics — August 17, 2008**: Representing Spain, Nadal won the Olympic men's singles gold medal, defeating Fernando González of Chile in the final 6–3, 7–6(2), 6–3. The victory completed a run through the Beijing draw without dropping a set in the later rounds and added an Olympic title to his 2008 haul.

**World No. 1 — August 18, 2008**: The Olympic result, combined with his earlier titles, moved Nadal to the top of the ATP rankings for the first time in his career, ending Federer's 237-week tenure at No. 1. Nadal finished the year as the year-end No. 1, though he did not add a hard-court major, losing in the semifinals of the US Open to Andy Murray.

## Significance

Nadal's 2008 season marked the decisive shift in what became one of the defining rivalries in the history of the sport. By winning on clay, grass, and hard courts in a single year and taking the No. 1 ranking, he demonstrated a versatility that his earlier reputation as a clay-court specialist had not anticipated, and he broke a period of Federer dominance that had persisted since early 2004.

The 2008 Wimbledon final in particular became a cultural touchstone beyond tennis, drawing large international audiences and repeatedly ranked in retrospectives as the finest match ever contested. Together with his French Open and Olympic titles, it established Nadal as a global sporting figure during a year — anchored by the Beijing Olympics — in which individual athletic achievement drew unusually wide attention.

Nadal's ascent in 2008 laid the foundation for a career that would ultimately include 22 Grand Slam singles titles, a record 14 French Open championships, and a second Olympic gold (in doubles at London 2012), cementing his standing among the most accomplished players in the history of the game.
